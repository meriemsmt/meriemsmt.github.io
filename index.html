Meriem Smati

PhD Candidate, INSA Lyon, Polytechnique Montréal
State Engineer in Computer Systems Engineering with a Master's Degree.

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science and Industrial Engineering attached to DISP laboratory (INSA Lyon, France) and Polytechnique Montréal (Canada), working at the intersection of Digital Twins, System of Systems, and resilience engineering. My research focuses on designing modular digital twin architectures that enhance the resilience of interconnected, complex systems, with applications ranging from small IoT cases to larger scales. I am currently developing methodologies to evaluate and improve the adaptive, recovery, and absorption capabilities of these systems, ensuring they can respond effectively to disruptions. More details in my CV.

My main research interests include:

  • Digital Twins and System of Systems
  • Resilience engineering and adaptive systems
  • IoT architectures and real-time data integration
  • Machine learning applications for predictive modeling

Previously, I have contributed to empirical projects involving the integration of digital twins for absorption and physical component failure in an IoT system (Demonstration Video - CSDT) as well as into waste collection systems and smart cities (Demonstration Video - WasteTwin), contributing to both the academic and concrete aspects to bridge the gap between the theoretical frameworks and practical implementations
